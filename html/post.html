A few of my favorites

An assortment of small poems, messy thoughts, and a tribute to the power of words.

By Amy McDaniel

inside the walls 5.11.20 It wasn’t just that we could no longer step into the outside world. It was that we had never seen the insides of our own homes with such stark clarity. The walls were closer than we remembered, the ceilings higher. We had not noticed that stain on the carpet, that scratch in the paint. There were more noises, too— the dog’s breathing, the mess of voices floating down the stairs, the incessant tapping of fingers on keyboards. The people were closer together, but somehow farther apart, too, everyone so occupied with what we were missing that sometimes we forgot to enjoy what we were getting. There were more microwave beeps and more doors opening and more dishes in the sink and more stubbed toes and anxious piano songs and computer screens and sighs of exhaustion. Sometimes we couldn’t stand the details and sometimes we couldn’t handle the nothingness of it all. But every morning we woke up, and every night we went to bed, and one day we realized that there was a strange sort of beauty to this world inside the walls, one we hadn’t taken the time to see before. wilderness 12.10.17 Crushed pine needles A blanket of sunlight and smoke Sweat against the cold Dirt under fingernails Dusty air in lungs Songs swirling in the wind Wildflowers and poison ivy Aching muscles Mountains to climb queen 1.9.18 Sungolden cathedrals Alabaster caverns Heartbeat in the ground. emily 1.9.18 She saw her words as living As breathing As human. But really, you broke your own heart— Hers is too pretty to bleed for long. I confess, I wish you had done it sooner, before I gave up, before you had lost your sincerity to the joking confidence that is only freckle-deep. But I don’t blame you. She talks more and laughs easily and smiles for all she sees. She does it better than I could, better than I want to. I know I’m still just a girl. I know you never thought of me as much as I thought of you. Can we at least be friends now? You haven’t talked to me since May. I miss your smile, your glances. I promise you won’t have to break your own heart because of me. I’m not much of a heartbreaker anyway. fake 6.24.19 The music and laughter hit me, hard and cold, in the face. I tried to turn around but couldn’t, so I stayed there, frozen amid the cacophony of noises. My own throat felt too tight, my laugh too loud, eyes too tired. seventeen 6.24.19 She still hasn’t kissed a boy or held someone’s hand or been to a concert or had a sleep-over. onward, onward, onward until the car parks and the hollowness encages her she wipes the tears from her eyelashes wrestles a breath into her aching lungs opens the door puts on her congratulations-I’m-so-happy-for-you face there are cousins and uncles and aunts sunflowers and blue ribbons little party sandwiches and ice cream I’m doing good how are you! tumbles so emptily from her teeth but no one questions it and in the family photos it won’t even look like a single tear has been shed. god 10.16.23 You wonder about God Who and where and why Was it just words they said And justifications they made And I suppose I’ve wondered too And sometimes wonder still But the periwinkle skies The spiced autumn air The morning sun breaking through my window A mother’s embrace A good friend’s laugh My lungs bursting after a run The light in my soul after teaching a lesson The joy of family The pain I have felt turn to healing It is too good to be coincidence Too beautiful to be an accident His hand is in the details And I see His face In you In me In all of us We are a family, this world Dysfunctional at times, yes But a family And that’s how I know who God is.
